Right now it is just a few fragment shaders. You can interact with the shaders by using the slider, or clicking and dragging on some of the images.

This shader calculates wether each pixel (x,y) represents the Roundest (most similar) Factors of x * y. Colored pixels are points that were just confirmed on the current loop of the algorithm, green for roundest and red for not. Grey is unconfirmed, white is confirmed true and black is confirmed false.

This shader shows a serial version of the Round Factor algorithm meant for regular CPU execution working to find the roundest factor of the pixel's x * y. When the roundest factors are found, the pixel is colored with red representing the differance between that pixel's position and the position of the roundest factor pair, and green and blue represent the absolure position of the roundest factor pair.

I made this to explore some of the patterns I saw in the round factor shaders.

The colors found on a bubble or oil film or on the surface of oxidized metal are caused by thin film interference. This shader simulates the colors produced by different thickness of the thin film. What ends up happening is that red green and blue have their brightnesses set to a sine wave with the wavelength based on their real wavelengths.

I was messing around and I just think this one looks neat.
